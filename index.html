Procore Construction Data Standard

An interactive map of how construction data connects — from budgets to beams, schedules to submittals, and everything in between.

Domains
A domain is a major category of construction data. Each one represents a distinct area of project management — money, time, people, physical work, or documentation.
Examples: Financial Instruments, Schedule, Building Elements, Field Operations
Elements
An element is a specific data object within a domain. Elements are the building blocks — the individual records, documents, and transactions that make up a project.
Examples: Change Order, Punch Item, Concrete Pour, RFI, Budget Line Item
Relationships
Relationships are the action verbs that connect elements across domains. They describe how data actually flows and interacts on a real construction project.
Examples: FUNDS, ERECTED BY, GOVERNS, SCHEDULES, LOGGED PER
